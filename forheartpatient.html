Foods

Choose foods with little to no added sugar, saturated fats, and sodium. To get enough protein throughout the day and maintain muscle, try adding seafood, dairy, or fortified soy products along with beans, peas, and lentils to your meals. Learn more about protein and other important nutrients.

Fruits

Fruit can be good for your general health, and many fruits have high levels of nutrients like fiber and polyphenols that benefit your heart.

Vegetables

A diet rich in vegetables and fruits can lower blood pressure, reduce the risk of heart disease and stroke, prevent some types of cancer.

Milk

The inclusion of dairy foods for cardiovascular health and lowering blood pressure levels, so you can feel confident making this recommendation to your patients. It's definitely time to celebrate milk as part of a balanced and nutritious diet.

Sea Food

Eating seafood provides a positive benefit for heart health. Heart disease is the leading cause of death in both men and women. Eating fish as little as once per week reduces the risk of death from coronary heart disease.
